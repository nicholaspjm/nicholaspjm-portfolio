about

Nicholas Marriott is a creative technologist based in Naarm / Melbourne, b. 1999 in Aotearoa New Zealand. He works in real-time systems as a visual designer, performer, and exhibiting artist, across of variety of mediums.

His visuals have featured on major stages and screens, including The xx's 2026 festival tour and a Grammy winning music video for Nine Inch Nails, alongside commercial work for Nike and live performances at festivals including Pitch Music & Arts. His installation work extends these systems into physical space, most recently a large scale reactive light environment for Chadstone. His practice has been profiled in It's Nice That and Rolling Stone.

I’m available for commissions, art direction, teaching, and speaking.

public tools ~/practice/released

  • 01/08Woven Touch (TouchDesigner · GLSL) ↗
    A TouchDesigner instrument that weaves text, images, and 3D geometry into drooping woven thread. Real time, with gravity and click-drag interaction.
  • 02/08lumen (TypeScript · Art-Net · DMX) ↗
    Live-coding environment for DMX lighting rigs. Write patterns live and the lights follow on the next beat.
  • 03/08blob tracker (TouchDesigner · OpenCV) ↗
    TouchDesigner-native blob tracking component for interactive floors and walls.
  • 04/08web blob tracker (JavaScript · Web) ↗
    The blob-tracker detection look running natively in the browser, no TouchDesigner required.
  • 05/08wiki visualiser (TypeScript · WebGL) ↗
    Turns Wikipedia into real-time visual maps from articles, link graphs, and live edits. Co-developed with Steven Croker; download via their Patreon.
  • 06/08datamosh (TouchDesigner · GLSL) ↗
    Datamoshing as an instrument. Compression-artifact glitching made controllable and performable.
  • 07/08palette chooser (TouchDesigner · Python) ↗
    Real-time palette extraction using k-means colour analysis, turning any image or live feed into usable swatch palettes.
  • 08/08TD-Notes-Tools (TouchDesigner · Python) ↗
    Two external .tox note panels for TouchDesigner: a notebook and a sticky canvas that link to and jump to nodes.
